Chapter 2: The Red String

Fate at Hand

Dear Taliah,

They say there's an invisible string that connects two people who are meant to be in each other's lives. You can't see it, but it's always there. Soft, quiet, patient. It stretches across cities, oceans, and lifetimes. Never breaking, only waiting for the right time to pull you close. The universe keeps two people apart until the time is right. Not out of cruelty or love, but out of care. It wants you both to grow, to heal, to understand what love truly means before you meet.

Maybe we noticed the string and yanked it too soon. Because if you meet too soon, you pass each other by, too lost in our own storms to notice the calm in each other's eyes. And still the string waits, tangled in time, letting you wander, fall and rise again. It stays steady while you learn. And when the universe decides the moment has finally come, the string gives you a gentle pull, just enough to guide two souls back to where they belong. No rush, no noise, just a quiet crossing of paths that feels like home. Because the universe doesn't follow our clocks. It follows something deeper, something written in stars stitched with fate. And no matter how far you go, that invisible string will always lead you right to where life is meant to find you.

- Vaughan

Chapter 3: The Little Moments

Sometimes the smallest moments mean the most.

Dear Taliah,

Something I always look back on with fond memories are the little moments and inside jokes we shared.

From the movie tickets, curse coupons, "all these people", the lore updates, sitting in silence whilst the other works, the calls whilst getting ready, the NEIGHS, the Donnie Thornberry teasing after misspeaking and even the spelling mistake banter. That was when I would be at my happiest. That is the smile I would do anything for. That is the little moment that felt bigger than even some of our big moments. That face, smile and feeling is something I will never and honestly, can't ever forget.

Chapter 4: Lessons

Growing and Learning from Mistakes

Dear Taliah,

Love is how we treat someone. Not how we feel about them. That's a lesson I learned way too late.

I had it all backwards. I thought love was this big feeling, this rush, this thing that happens to us. I said "I Love You" and thought that was enough. Like the words carry weight on their own. Like just saying the words means I did my part. I got so caught up in the feelings, the butterflies, the "I Love You's" that I forgot that love is actually shown in the smallest, most boring moments. When you're exhausted but you still ask how their day was and actually listen. When you remember how they like their toast, even though liking it cold with unmelted butter is odd to meüëÄ. When you wake them up when you'd rather sleep in. Because you can feel everything for someone and still make them feel like nothing. I can have all the love in my heart but if my actions don't match it, what's the point? You didn't feel my feelings. You felt how I treated you. Love isn't just what happens in your chest. It's what happens in your hands, your words, your presence. It's the choice you make when the feelings get quiet, life gets hard and showing up feels inconvenient. That's when love actually matters.

And is the point of love not inconvenience? I understand that now. I want to go out of my way for the people I love. I want to put in the effort when it's hard. I want to show up even when I'm drained out. I don't want to be too scared of bothering my loved ones. Because loving means showing up when it's hard. When it's inconvenient.

- Vaughan
